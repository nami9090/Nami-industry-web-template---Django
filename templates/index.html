Background

The migrations of people from Uvira land which comprise the ancestral kingdoms of Fuliiru and Vira people to North America for the past three decades has left a cultural vacuum in the minds of one generation of young men and women. There is an attempt to affirm that this vacuum goes two generations back based on life experiences both in Canada and the USA.

The recent Canadians and Americans of Fuliiru-Vira ascendance , not only are losing the taste of their ancestral languages and traditions, they are also loosing trace of their artifacts, Proverbs, arts and sculptures inherited from their forefathers. In addition to this cultural change, indifference and ignorance, those who tried to lift up the banner through education, history telling and public services are even absent in the memories of today generations. NAFV INT. is dedicated to collect, protect and preserve these cultural writings, objects, photographs, audio and recordings, artworks and other social customs.

Have a question? Check out the FAQ

Of course! Here's the English version of the FAQ about the Bafuliru and Bavira tribes:

Who are the Bafuliru and Bavira?

The Bafuliru and Bavira are two Bantu ethnic groups living mainly in the Uvira region of South Kivu, in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC). They share similar linguistic roots but maintain distinct cultural identities.

What are the main cultural differences between the Fuliru and the Vira?

Although they live in nearby areas, the Fuliru are more traditionally involved in agriculture and trade, while the Vira are historically known for their social hierarchy and cattle herding practices.

Do the Fuliru and Vira speak the same language?

They speak closely related languages: Kifuliiru for the Fuliru and Kivira for the Vira. These languages are mutually intelligible but differ slightly in pronunciation and vocabulary.

What is the role of traditional chiefs among the Bafuliru and Bavira?

Traditional leaders play a key role in managing community affairs, resolving conflicts, and preserving customs. Each tribe has its own respected chieftaincy structure.

What challenges do the Fuliru and Vira face today?

Both communities face challenges such as land disputes, political instability in the region, economic underdevelopment, and the struggle to preserve their cultural heritage in a modernizing world.

What are some important traditional ceremonies or festivals among the Fuliru and Vira?

Both tribes hold rites of passage like initiation into adulthood, traditional weddings, and harvest festivals. These events are celebrated with dances, songs, and rituals specific to each group.
